## Second Renaissance Forum — launch announcement

---

**Early-stage: starting a knowledge base on collective action and cooperation**

I'm planning to run a course on collective action and cooperation later this year, and I've started building a knowledge base / wiki as part of the prep for that. Sharing here in case it's useful or sparks conversation.

The topic: the gap between what groups *could* achieve by coordinating, and what they actually manage to achieve — and how central this seems to so many of the big challenges and opportunities we face right now. Very early days — right now it's little more than a README and a note on the Stag Hunt — but the intention is to build it up over time into a useful resource on the key concepts, frameworks, and real-world applications.

The framing I'm working with is less "collective action *problems*" (the classic economic framing) and more "collective action and cooperation *opportunities*" — starting from the recognition that the potential gains from coordination are often enormous, and asking: what makes cooperation possible, and what gets in the way?

Applications I'm particularly interested in: governing powerful technologies (AI, nuclear), climate and environmental commons, information ecosystems.

I'm also planning to turn this into a short course at some point this year.

You can see what I've got so far here: https://github.com/life-itself/collective-action

Very much a work in progress — contributions welcome, whether that's adding notes, suggesting key concepts, or pointing to good resources. Would also love to hear if others here are thinking about related things.
